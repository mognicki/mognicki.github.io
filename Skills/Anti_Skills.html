Skills Anti-Skills

Anti-Skills

I want to be honest, that’s why I decided to make this list of anti-skills - skills/technologies/etc I don’t like, I tried to like and it didn’t work out or I don’t even want to try on.

It doesn’t mean I’m the enemy of following, they’re just not my cup of tea.

WordPress

I used to be a WordPress developer back in the day, but now, I’m out. However, I acknowledge the importance of WordPress as an Internet standard and won’t spend hours trying to convince its supporters to think over their lives again.

Symfony

My area of expertise is Laravel. I’ve seen lots of quarrels between Laravel and Symfony followers, but I won’t involve in any discussion. The fact that I excel at Laravel somehow makes my Symfony mastering unnecessary. I’d choose one framework and stick to it.

YAGNI principle

My coding MO contradicts the YAGNI principle. It doesn’t mean, though, that I spend hours developing features that will never be employed. It means that whatever I do, I try to think of possible extensions, therefore leaving some empty slots for future development.
