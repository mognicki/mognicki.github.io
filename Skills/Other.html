Communication skills

I value good communication, not only on the professional level but in private life as well. It’s something I learned studying sociology, that as every person is different, safe and open communication plays a key role in social interactions.

Interdisciplinarity

I’m interdisciplinary, which means that I usually walk miles beyond the strict IT knowledge when challenged with complex problems. Example? Asked to provide Laravel-specific solutions for Norway shipping, I naturally oversaw possible risks (like variation of shipping costs due to the country meridian extent) and developed out-of-the-box ideas (such as using qGIS for determination of remote areas).

Problem solving

If you ask any of my friends who to go if you want your problem solved, that’d probably be me. I use all my interdisciplinary skills, together with out-of-the-box thinking to get rid of all riddles I encounter.

Leadership

That’s no skill I learnt in the IT industry. That’s the skill I learned and mastered during my involvement in the students’ movement. My responsibilities included organizing and managing projects of the inter-faculty level, and the good leadership skills were essential for the projects to succeed.

I believe that leadership is not only, well, leading, but mostly looking for the team members’ best values.
